Exponential distribution in R

Author: D.Aiazzi

Overview

We investigate the exponential distribution in R. With the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) we compare the theoretical population distribution simulated by ‘rexp()’ with the distribution of 1000 samples of 40 exponentials.
In particular we want to:
1. Show the sample mean and compare it to the theoretical mean of the distribution.
2. Show how variable the sample is (via variance) and compare it to the theoretical variance of the distribution.
3. Show that the distribution is approximately normal.

Simulation

We assume X to be an exponential distribution with lambda \(\lambda\) = 0.2, therefore \(\mu\) = 1/\(\lambda\) and sd = 1/\(\lambda\)

1. Theoretical versus sample mean

In the exponential distribution the theoretical mean is \(\mu\) = 1/\(\lambda\), that is:

## [1] 5

The sample mean {X} is very close to the theoretical value and it is:

## [1] 4.997191

2. Theoretical versus sample variance

The theoretical variance of the exponential distribution is Var[X] = 1/\(\lambda^{2}\), that is:

## [1] 25

The sample variance Var[\(\bar{X}\)] is:

## [1] 0.6440288

3. Distribution

In point 3, we will focus on the difference between the distribution of a large collection of random exponentials and the distribution of a large collection of averages of 40 exponentials. The CLT tells us that \(\sf{\bar{X}_{n}}\) ~ N(\(\mu\), \(\sigma^2\)/n), that is the averages of n samples approximates a normal distribution with mean \(\mu\) and variation \(\sigma^2\)/n.
In the following plot, the distribution of the averages of samples is normalised. The histograms and blue line rapresent the normalised distribution of the averages of samples from the simulation, the green dotted line rapresents a standard normal distribution.

Appendix

The code chunks

####SIMULATION####
set.seed(100)
#exponential distribution
lambda <- 0.2
distr_size <- 1000
exponentials <- rexp(distr_size, rate = lambda)
#matrix of 1000 simulations of samples of size 40
n_sim <- 1000
sample_size <- 40
m <- matrix(rexp(n_sim*sample_size, rate = lambda), nrow = n_sim, ncol = sample_size)
#vector of 1000 averages of samples
sample_averages <- apply(m, 1, mean)

plot(density(exponentials), 
     lwd = 2, 
     main = "Probability Density Function of an exponential distribution\n (lambda = 0.2)",
     xlab = "Values",
     ylab = "Density")
abline(v = mean(exponentials), col = "red")
text(x = mean(exponentials) + 5, 
     y = 0.14,
     col = "red", 
     labels = "mu = 5")

#### 1. Theoretical versus sample mean ####
mu <- 1/lambda
mean(sample_averages)

#### 2. Theoretical versus sample variance ####
1/(lambda^2)
var(sample_averages)

#### 3. Distribution ####

sample_averages_norm <- (sample_averages - mu)/var(sample_averages)#normalisation of the averages of samples
hist(sample_averages_norm, 
     breaks = 50, 
     col = "grey", 
     prob = TRUE, 
     main = "Normalised distribution of averages of samples",
     xlab = "")
lines(density(sample_averages_norm), col = "blue", lwd = 2)
abline(v=mean(sample_averages_norm), col="red", lwd = 3)
lines(density(rnorm(1000000)), lty = 2, lwd = 3, col = "darkgreen")
legend('topright', 
       c("theoretical", "simulation"), 
       col = c("darkgreen", "blue"), 
       lty = c(2,1), 
       lwd = 3, 
       bty = "n")
